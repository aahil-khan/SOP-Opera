# Pipeline audit and remediation — 2026-07-22

An end-to-end audit of the SOP Opera pipeline against two questions: **are we doing
what we claim**, and **is this the right architecture**. This document records what
was found, what was changed, and why — including the judgement calls and the things
deliberately *not* done.

Working plan: `~/.claude/plans/piped-dreaming-aho.md`. Six workstreams, W1–W6.

---

## Executive summary

The system was better engineered than its claims were defensible. The LangGraph
fan-out, the `SKIP LOCKED` queue, the routing gates, the networkx proximity graph
and the FSM/audit spine were all genuine work. The problems were concentrated in
the layer we led with.

**The single most serious finding:** the headline evaluation metric was circular.
Ground-truth labels were defined as *"where the compound detector fires"*, so the
reported 0% false-negative rate was true by construction and unfalsifiable. A
technically literate judge opening one file would have seen it in about thirty
seconds, and that discovery would have discredited every other number.

Secondary findings: lead time was measured in demo playback seconds rather than
plant process time; the "compound engine" was a fact counter (`len(facts) >= 3`);
several Indian regulatory citations were fabricated in ways a domain expert would
catch on sight; "immutable audit trail" was an ordinary table with no integrity
mechanism; and roughly 900 lines of unreachable provider scaffolding was kept alive
by four tests that silently asserted nothing.

**Headline numbers, before and after:**

| Metric | Before (circular) | After (independent labels) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Dataset size | 17 cases, 6 positive | 377 cases, 257 positive |
| Single-sensor FN rate | 66.7% | 33.9% |
| Compound FN rate | 0.0% *(by construction)* | 0.0% *(measured, 8 FP)* |
| Compound precision | 85.7% | 97.0% |
| VSP lead time | "18 seconds" (playback) | **28 minutes** (process time) |
| Regulatory coverage | *no metric existed* | 100% citable / 91.1% statutory |

## Part 1 — What the audit found

Findings were verified by reading code and by *running* it, not inferred. Four
parallel subagents covered geospatial, regulatory/RAG, the agent architecture, and
scalability; the eval harness was audited directly because that was where the
credibility risk concentrated.

### F1 — The headline metric was circular *(fatal if opened)*

`eval/dataset.py:49`, `_dangerous_at_step()`. The docstring was the admission:
*"When compound fusion should reach blocking before single-sensor critical."*
Labels for the 11 timeline cases were literally `step_index >= 2` (vsp) and
`>= 3` (compound_risk) — chosen to be exactly where `compound_alarm()` fired.

Verified by running the harness: across all 17 cases the compound detector
disagreed with its own ground truth on **exactly one**. The 0% FN rate was
definitional, not measured. 17 cases / 6 positives also cannot support
three-significant-figure percentages.

### F2 — The compound policy existed in three hand-synced copies

- `_fuse_risk` — `agents/nodes/orchestrator.py` (the shipped verdict)
- `_risk_level` — `assessment/providers/mock.py` (dead, still drifting)
- `_is_blocking_compound` — `reviews/service.py`, comment: *"Mirror orchestrator blocking gate"*

`eval/detectors.py` imported `_fuse_risk` **from an agent node** and called it with
an empty-observation signature production never used. Production `grounded` also
folded in `predicted_trend_risk` and supervisor facts that the detector's did not.
**The measured detector and the shipped verdict could disagree on the same input.**

### F3 — The "compound engine" was a fact counter

`if COMPOUND_TRIO.issubset(rule_facts) or len(rule_facts) >= 3: return "blocking"`.
Any three unrelated facts blocked — weather hold + expiring certification + PPE gap
→ BLOCK. No notion of hazard pathway.

Related invariant break: `predictive_trend` emitted `predicted_trend_risk`, which is
not in `DERIVED_FACT_RULES`, yet counted toward the `>= 3` gate and satisfied
`require_grounding_for_block`. The stated invariant *"BLOCK must be backed by ≥1
deterministic rule fact"* was violable by a projection — including by the sparse-data
heuristic fallback.

### F4 — "Predictive ML forecast" ran on a fabricated time axis

Every entry in an eval case shared one `valid_from`. Verified: a 5-entry case had
**1 distinct timestamp**. `_ols_fit` therefore hit its degenerate branch and
substituted a synthetic 1-sample-per-second cadence. The slope, r², and
`seconds_to_elevated` behind the forecast row measured index position, not a trend.

### F5 — "18s lead time" was demo playback pacing

`eval/lead_time.py` summed `delay_seconds` from the scenario YAML — simulator replay
delays (0,4,4,8,10s). The number described how fast the demo played, not how far
ahead of an incident the system warned. *"18 seconds before a coke-oven explosion"*
invites a fatal follow-up question.

### F6 — Regulatory citations were fabricated

The corpus was 36 hardcoded rows; the Indian pack was 5 regulations + 2 SOPs of
one-line paraphrase with no clause text.

- `Factory Act 1948 §41` — not a real citation shape (hazardous-process provisions are §41A–41H); the quoted duty was invented.
- `DGMS Circular 2017` — no number or series, and **DGMS has no jurisdiction over a steel-plant coke oven** (that is the Factories Act inspectorate).
- `OISD-STD-117` labelled "Permit to Work" — 117 is Fire Protection Facilities; the work-permit standard is OISD-STD-105.

There was also **no regulatory coverage metric anywhere** — the strongest claim in
the deck was the only one with no number behind it.

### F7 — "RAG over a regulatory corpus" was deterministic SQL

- `EMBEDDING_PROVIDER` defaults to `mock` = `sha256(text)` expanded to 1536 floats — a random unit vector. Measured cosines on real corpus text: **0.0127, −0.0216, 0.0222**, against a 0.72 gate. RAG graded `weak` on *every* query, so the deterministic path always won.
- `RAG_VECTOR_SOURCE_TYPES = ["historical_incidents"]` — **regulations and SOPs were never vector-searched in any config.** Regulatory retrieval was `WHERE applies_to_category = :cat LIMIT 5` with Indian codes floated by a `LIKE 'OISD%'` string prefix.
- No validation that a clause named in a summary existed in the corpus. The only enforcement was a prompt line saying "never invent references."

Guaranteed-citation determinism is a *defensible engineering choice*. Calling it RAG
was not.

### F8 — Geospatial was real geometry, excluded from every metric

`graph/kg.py` is a genuine networkx graph (53 nodes; 3 `NEAR`, 21 `ABOVE`, 27
`LOCATED_IN` edges) with real Euclidean adjacency. But `eval/detectors.py` passed
`observations=[]`, so `spatial_hit` was structurally always `False` — **every
headline number was computed with the spatial subsystem switched off.**

In the hero VSP scenario spatial was decorative anyway: all steps are on one asset,
so `len >= 3` fired before the spatial branch was reached, and same-asset distance
is 0.0 m — a co-location tautology.

Also: the floor plan is a checked-in JSON hand-duplicated into `frontend/lib/`
outside `sync-shared.mjs`; `agent_scale_m_per_px = 0.04` makes the entire steel
plant 66 m × 56 m; `rule_zone_occupied` matches the literal string `"hazardous"` and
never compares the worker's zone to the asset's zone; and `frontend/lib/riskHeatmap.ts`
contains no heatmap despite CLAUDE.md claiming one.

### F9 — The default clone was 100% templates

`AI_PROVIDER=mock` → `get_chat_model()` returns `None` → **zero network calls**.
Only 3 LLM call sites existed in the whole backend; 4 of 9 agents could never call
an LLM under any config; the LLM wrote exactly one field (`summary`). Our local
`.env` was `openai_compatible`, so **our demo was not what a fresh clone produced.**

Consequences: `graph.py` validated a dict the code had constructed three lines
earlier (cannot fail); `assessment/providers/` was unreachable; and four test files
monkeypatched `app.assessment.pipeline.get_provider`, **an attribute that does not
exist**, so they asserted nothing.

### F10 — Durability, integrity and load ceilings

- **No lease / visibility timeout.** An exception after generation left the row `generating` and the review `assessing` until process restart. `recover_pending()` ran only at boot.
- **`_provider_override` was a module-level global** with 2 workers by default.
- **`enqueue_for_review` was check-then-insert** with no partial unique index.
- **Broadcast had no backpressure** — sequential in-line `await send_json`, so one stalled tab throttled telemetry plant-wide and stalled the ambient loop.
- **`GET /reviews` had no LIMIT** despite being the most-called endpoint, against a default 5+10 connection pool while a job holds one connection for its entire agent run.
- **No ANN index** on `knowledge_chunks.embedding`; **no index** on `assessments(status)` under a table-wide UPDATE every poll tick.
- **"Immutable audit trail" was an ordinary table** — no hash chain, no enforcement. Evidence froze context **IDs, not content**, so it dangled if rows changed.

## Part 2 — What was changed

### W1 — Single risk policy, hazard-pathway model

**New: `backend/app/risk/policy.py`** — the one home for compound-risk logic,
importing nothing from `agents/`, `reviews/` or `eval/`. All three now delegate to
it, so the shipped verdict and the measured verdict cannot drift.

The fact counter was replaced with a **hazard-pathway model**. Each derived fact
supplies a hazard *dimension*:

| Dimension | Facts |
| --- | --- |
| `atmosphere` | elevated/critical gas, effluent breach, tank level critical |
| `ignition_energy` | over/critical temperature, vibration anomaly, incomplete isolation |
| `exposure` | zone occupied, PPE noncompliance, lifting conflict |
| `control_failure` | incomplete isolation, permit conflict, simultaneous ops, cert expiring, weather hold |

Blocking requires the single-sensor critical line, a supervisor safety hazard, or a
genuine *pathway* — the full atmosphere+ignition+control chain, personnel exposed to
a hazardous atmosphere, incompatible simultaneous operations, or ≥3 complementary
dimensions with exposure.

`predicted_trend_risk` and `spatial_cooccurrence` became explicit **non-grounding
signals**: they may escalate to `elevated` and strengthen an already-grounded block,
but can never ground one. `RiskVerdict` carries `triggered_rule`, `dimensions`,
`rationale` and `POLICY_VERSION`, so every verdict can say which clause fired.

`FACT_RECOMMENDATIONS` moved to `app/risk/recommendations.py`.

**Consequence accepted by the user:** the hero scenario now blocks one step *earlier*
(t+6 min, elevated gas + unverified hot-work isolation) rather than waiting for the
worker to enter. The pathway is complete without anyone present, which is what a
work-permit standard actually requires — and it lengthens the demonstrated lead time.

### W2 — Independent ground truth, real time, larger dataset

**New: `backend/app/eval/hazard_ground_truth.py`** — labels each case by asking
*"does a statutory stop-work provision apply to this plant state?"*, evaluated over
**raw context payloads**. It never imports the risk policy and never calls the rule
engine.

**New: `backend/tests/test_eval_independence.py`** — enforces this structurally. It
AST-parses the ground-truth module and fails if it imports `app.risk` or calls
`classify`/`evaluate_rules`. It *also* fails if labels and detector ever agree on
**every** case, because perfect agreement is the signature of circularity returning.

`eval/dataset.py` was rebuilt: a parameter sweep over gas level × trajectory ×
permit/isolation configuration × personnel × temperature, plus scenario timelines →
**377 cases at a 68/32 class balance**, each carrying a `label_rationale` and the
clause it rests on.

Timestamps became physical. Scenario steps gained `t_offset_minutes` (process time)
alongside `delay_seconds` (playback pacing). Sensor samples are minutes apart, so
`_ols_fit` no longer falls into its synthetic-cadence branch. **Lead time is now
reported in process minutes**; API, frontend and landing page were updated.

**The honest dataset immediately found two real bugs in the new policy** — 33 false
negatives:

- 17 cases: a worker in a hazardous atmosphere with no ignition source. The pathway model required 3 dimensions; gassing needs only atmosphere + exposure. *This is the core life-safety case and it had been missed.*
- 16 cases: hot work concurrent with confined-space entry. SIMOPS is a stop condition on its own.

Both were fixed — justified by the regulation, not by the metric. The 8 remaining
false positives were **left in place**: they are cases where the engine is
deliberately stricter than the statutory minimum, and erasing them by tuning the
labels would have re-introduced exactly the circularity being removed.

### W3 — Verified citations, citation validation, coverage metric

Every clause was **checked against primary sources** rather than written from
memory, because wrong citations were the original defect.

| Was (invented) | Now (verified) |
| --- | --- |
| `Factory Act 1948 §41` | **Factories Act 1948 s.37(1)** — verbatim (a)(b)(c), incl. *"exclusion or effective enclosure of all possible sources of ignition"* |
| `DGMS Circular 2017` | **dropped** — no such citation, and no coke-oven jurisdiction |
| `OISD-STD-117` "Permit to Work" | **OISD-STD-105** Work Permit System (Rev. I, Sept 2004) |
| — | **s.36(2)** confined-space entry (competent person's certificate) |
| — | **s.41H** imminent danger → immediate remedial action |

s.37(1)(c) is the compound-risk thesis stated in statute — a stronger pitch line
than anything the deck previously had.

`regulations` gained `clause` and `source_url` columns, threaded through
`RetrievedReference` to the UI, so a citation is checkable rather than trustable.
Retrieval ordering became semantic (*has a clause + source URL*) instead of
`code LIKE 'OISD%'`, which had silently stopped matching once codes were corrected.

**New: `backend/app/assessment/citations.py`** — extracts citation-shaped tokens
from generated prose and checks them against what was actually retrieved.
Unsupported citations are stripped and logged as an agent step.

**New: `backend/app/eval/coverage.py`** — the rubric line that had no number:
**100%** of fact-bearing cases have a citable regulation, **91.1%** an Indian
statutory provision, broken down by standard, in the report and on the API.

Also removed: the hardcoded `score: 0.91` on offline incident echoes (a fabricated
similarity beside fabricated text was the most audit-fragile object in the demo),
and the SOP that baked `OISD-GDN-116` / `DGMS` into its body.

### W4 — Honest defaults, dead tier deleted, dead tests repaired

Deleted the entire `assessment/providers/` package (mock/ollama/openai_compatible,
the Protocol and `get_provider`) plus its two test files. `agents/llm.py
get_chat_model()` was already the only live seam.

**Four silently-dead tests repaired.** `test_notifications`, `test_failure_reason`
(×2), `test_manual_assessment`, `test_retry_endpoint` all monkeypatched a
non-existent attribute. They now patch `pipeline.run_agent_assessment`.

Fixing `test_retry_endpoint` surfaced a second latent bug: it failed only *one*
generation call, but the pipeline retries internally, so attempt 2 succeeded, the
job never reached `failed`, and the retry endpoint had nothing to retry — its core
assertion was unreachable.

The UI now reads **"deterministic narration · no LLM configured"** in mock mode
instead of implying reasoning. `docs/pitch-scorecard.md` was rewritten around real
numbers with an *"if a judge pushes"* section; CLAUDE.md no longer claims a risk
heatmap that does not exist or DGMS coverage that should not.

**Deviation from plan, deliberate:** the validate/retry loop was kept. The plan
called it vestigial because it re-runs an identical deterministic call — true in
mock mode, but with a real LLM `run_agent_assessment` genuinely fails on timeouts
and network errors, so retrying is real resilience.

### W5 — Scalability

- **Lease reclaim.** Claims stamp `claimed_at`; expired leases (`assessment_lease_seconds`, default 300s) are reclaimed on the next poll instead of stranding until restart.
- **DB-enforced idempotency.** Partial unique index on `assessments(review_id) WHERE status IN ('pending','generating')` + `ON CONFLICT DO NOTHING`, replacing check-then-insert.
- **Provider override moved onto the job row** — it would have silently broken the moment anyone ran `--workers N`.
- **Bounded broadcast.** Per-client queue and writer task; `broadcast()` never awaits a socket. Drop-oldest on overflow (correct here: every event triggers a client refetch, so newer supersedes older). Depth and `ws_dropped_frames` surfaced on AI Ops.
- **Query ceilings.** Bounded `GET /reviews` (default 200, `?limit=` to 1000); pool raised to 20+10 with pre-ping; added `idx_assessments_status` and an `ivfflat` ANN index on `knowledge_chunks.embedding`.

The new unique index caught a real problem: `test_orchestrator_recovery` was
constructing a pending *and* a generating row for the same review — a state the
queue always assumed impossible and now cannot happen.

### W6 — Tamper-evident audit trail

**New: `backend/app/audit/chain.py`** — every entry hashes its own content together
with the previous entry's hash. Any edit, deletion or reordering breaks the chain,
and `GET /audit/verify` (**new: `app/audit/routes.py`**) reports the exact `seq` and
reason (`content_altered` vs `broken_link`).

Design details worth studying:
- Appends take a **transaction-scoped advisory lock**, so concurrent writers cannot read the same tail and fork the chain.
- **`seq` (BIGSERIAL) orders the chain**, not `recorded_at` — clock skew cannot reorder it.
- Pre-chain rows are **counted and reported, not flagged as tampering**, and do not reset the chain.
- The claim is stated carefully: this does not *prevent* tampering (a database owner can always rewrite rows), it makes tampering **detectable**.

Evidence now freezes **content**, not just IDs — context payloads, the assessment as
it stood, and a `snapshot_hash`.

**Writing the tests found a bug in the new code:** the first `verify_rows` carried
the *stored* hash forward, which let a tampered entry heal the chain behind itself —
contradicting the module's own docstring. It now carries the recomputed hash forward.

### Geospatial — reframed rather than forced *(user decision)*

Rather than wire spatial into the eval, it was reframed as a deliberate position:
geospatial is **evidence quality for the supervisor, not a scored detector input**.

The reasoning, recorded in `docs/pitch-scorecard.md` and CLAUDE.md: the stop-work
criteria are written about plant state, not tag topology, so scoring geometry
against them would mean labeling those cases to match our own spatial agent — the
circular metric W2 removed. And at the current floor-plan scale there are 3
same-floor `NEAR` edges with a single-asset hero scenario, so a with/without-KG
column would be a rounding error dressed as a result.

The `observations` parameter on `compound_alarm` was kept, with a docstring stating
plainly that the harness passes none and why.

## Part 5 — What remains

**Documented rather than changed**

`rule_zone_occupied` reads `worker_location.payload["zone"] == "hazardous"` — a
hazard classification reported by the workforce system, not a comparison against
`assets.zone`. The two share a name and mean different things (hazard class vs
plant-area label), which reads as a bug on first encounter; the rule now carries a
docstring saying so.

It was left as-is deliberately. `rule_*` functions are pure over
`ContextEntryView` by design, so a real proximity check means threading asset
metadata into the rule layer and giving that up. The change would also stop
`zone_occupied` firing in the VSP scenario (worker reports `hazardous`, Vessel A's
zone is `coke-oven-battery`), which removes the exposure dimension from the hero
demo and puts all 377 eval cases and every published number back into flux —
for no externally visible gain. Estimated 2–4 hours, mostly re-verification.

**Known gaps, stated in the pitch deck rather than hidden**

- `POST /api/ingest/webhook` is unauthenticated, with no idempotency key or rate limit. *Explicitly excluded from W5 scope by the user.*
- Workers are in-process asyncio tasks. The queue is now safe across processes, but the ambient simulator still starts once per process, so `--workers N` would produce N telemetry generators.
- Geospatial is not a scored detector input (deliberate — see above).

**Two claims still load-bearing but unverified**

1. **Exact statutory wording.** Clause text was transcribed from Indian Kanoon, not the Gazette. Section numbers and titles are solid; the wording deserves a domain check before the pitch.
2. **The fresh-clone path has never been run.** `./scripts/run-linux.sh` from a clean `.env.example` is what a judge does, and neither the audit nor the plan's verification section actually executed it. This is the highest-value remaining check.

**One pre-existing bug, not introduced here**

`test_blocked_decision_locks_asset_until_unblock_task_completion` fails with
`401 Not authenticated` on `/tasks/{id}/done` — the test client never sets an actor
cookie. Confirmed pre-existing by stashing all session work and re-running.

## Part 6 — Transferable lessons

1. **A metric that cannot fail is not a metric.** The strongest signal of circularity is perfect agreement. The independence test now fails the build if labels and detector *ever* agree on every case — a self-defence mechanism, not just a check.

2. **Honest measurement immediately pays for itself.** Within minutes of getting real labels, the dataset found two genuine holes in the brand-new risk model — including personnel exposure to a hazardous atmosphere, the core life-safety case.

3. **Resist tuning labels to flatter the detector.** The 8 false positives were left in. Erasing them would have quietly rebuilt the circularity being removed.

4. **Verify external facts against sources, not memory.** Wrong citations were the original defect; fixing them from recall would have reproduced it in a new form.

5. **Tests that patch a non-existent attribute pass while asserting nothing.** Four did. Worth a periodic grep for `monkeypatch.setattr` targets that no longer resolve.

6. **Two clocks need two names.** Conflating simulator playback pacing with plant process time turned a 28-minute safety margin into an unimpressive "18 seconds".

7. **Say what a thing is not.** "Detectable, not prevented" for the audit chain; "criterion coverage, not generalization" for the eval; "deterministic SQL, not RAG" for retrieval. Each is more defensible than the overclaim it replaced.
